History

    The Yu-gi-oh franchise started out as a manga in 1996. It tells the adventure of Yugi Mutou who solved the ancient Millennium Puzzle, which lends him the spirit of a nameless Pharaoh. From then, whenever Yugi or his friends encounter dangers, the spirit challenges the opposition to a Shadow Game that reveals one's heart. As the story progresses, Yugi and his friends venture through dangers to help the Pharaoh regains his memory and his true name.

    Yu-gi-oh card game have been around since 1999 in Japan and 2002 in North America. The game features thousands of cards, several archetypes, and an endless number of combo combinations. It involves 2 players, each has a main deck (40-60 cards) and an extra deck (max 15 cards). Each side of the field has 5 monster slots and 5 trap/spell slots, a field spell slot, a graveyard slot and 2 shared extra deck slots. The game has 2 different rulings: Trading Card Game (TCG) and Original Card Game (OCG), with TCG referring to North America and OCG referring to Japan. Having different rulings and regulations, the dynamics of each focuses on different aspects of the game.

Gameplay

  • The game begins by a round of rock, paper, scissor, or a coin flip to decide who goes first.
  • The board is set up as shown. Each player begins with 8000 Life Points, and alternate turns each. The game usually ends when a player's Life Points reaches 0.
  • Each turn consists of 6 phases: Draw Phase, Standby Phase, Main Phase 1, Battle Phase, Main Phase 2, and End Phase
  • The board consists of 5 slots for monster cards, 2 slots for link monsters, 5 slots for trap/spell cards, 1 slot for main deck, 1 slot for extra deck, 1 slot for graveyard (containing defeated monsters) 1 slot for banished cards, and 1 slot for field spell.
  • There are 3 main types of cards in the game: monster, trap, and spell. (browse below)

Monster Card

    Monster card is the main damage dealer in the game. There are several types of monster cards (regular cards, XYZ cards, Link cards, Synchro cards, etc). Each card has a name at the top, an element on the right of it, a level (amount of stars) below, an artwork, and types, effects, attack, defense below. When played, a monster card can be in either attack (placed vertical) or defense (placed horizontally) position. When first plays, if the card is in attack position, it must be facing up, and if in defense position then facing down. When destroyed, it will be moved to graveyard.

Spell Card

    Spell card has a similar layout to monster card, except it does not have level but the text [Spell Card] instead, and does not have attack or defense stats. Spell card can be played immediately from hand or set on the field on the back row of the monster slots. If the card is a field spell, however, it will be placed on the field spell zone. Each player can have only 1 field spell on the board. In addition, spell card is colored in teal. When destroyed, it will be moved to graveyard.

Trap Card

    Trap card is similar to spell card, it does not have level but the text [Trap Card] instead and does not have attack or defense stats either. Trap card cannot be played directly (unless specified on the card, these are called hand trap), it must be set in the trap/spell slots first. Trap card is colored in magenta. When destroyed, it will be moved to graveyard.
